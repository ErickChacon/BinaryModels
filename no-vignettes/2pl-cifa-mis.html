Multidimensional Two Parameter Logistic Model

Erick A. Chacon-Montalvan

2018-05-22

In this vignette, we show how to use the spifa package to fit a multidimensional 2 parameter logistic model used in item response theory. Let \(Y_{ij}\) be the response for item \(j\) in the individual \(i\). The model can be defined by using an auxiliary variable \(Z_{ij}\) such as \[\begin{align} {Y}_{ij} & = \left\lbrace \begin{array}[2]{cc} 1 & \text{if} ~ {Z}_{ij} > 0\\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{array} \right.\\ {Z}_{ij} & = c_j + a_j^\intercal\theta_i + \epsilon_{ij}, ~~ \epsilon_{ij} \sim {N}(0, 1)\\ {\theta}_i & \sim {N}({0}, {I}_m)\\ c_j & \sim {N}(0, \sigma_c^2)\\ {a}_j & \sim {N}(0, \sigma_a^2{I}_m)\\ \end{align}\]
